Bob Marley Museum

Home

One Love

Museum dedicated to the memory of the late Reggae superstar, Robert "Bob" Marley. The museum is located in Marley's original studio where he recorded many of his songs.
The museum is situated on the site of the legendary musician’s home, which he purchased in 1975. This house, featuring 19th-century architecture, was Marley’s home until his transition in 1981. It was converted into a museum six years later by his wife, Mrs Rita Marley. The main museum displays Marley’s personal treasures.
Relax and enjoy a sumptuous meal from the One Love Café after you’ve completed your tour which offers a peek into every aspect of Bob Marley’s life.

Devon House

Home

iScream

Sitting on 11 acres of property, located in the heart of the country’s capital, Kingston, is one of Jamaica’s finest and leading Heritage Sites.
This 127-year-old testimonial was built for Jamaica’s first black millionaire, Gorge Stiebel, the World Famous; Devon House!
Devon House is one of Jamaica’s most celebrated landmarks, due to its rich history and cultural diversity. It’s a Georgian style house that was built on a property once known as Devon Penn.
Oh yeah, it offers one of the best tasting ice-cream in the country.

Port Royal

Home

Wickedest City in the West

Port Royal is a village located at the end of the Palisadoes at the mouth of the Kingston Harbour, in southeastern Jamaica.
Founded in 1518 by the Spanish, it was once the largest city in the Caribbean, functioning as the centre of shipping and commerce in the Caribbean Sea by the latter half of the 17th century.
It was destroyed by an earthquake on June 7, 1692, which had an accompanying tsunami. Severe hurricanes have regularly damaged it. Another severe earthquake occurred in 1907.
The area has surrounding forts, offers scuba diving and tours around the environment.

Blue mountains

Home

Coffee and Trips

The Blue Mountains are the longest mountain range in Jamaica. From the summit, accessible via a walking track, both the north and south coasts of the island can be seen. On a clear day, the outline of the island of Cuba, 210 km (130 mi) away, can also be seen. The mountain range spans 4 parishes: Portland, St.Thomas, St Mary and St.Andrew.
In past years when Jamaica's economy was dominated by plantation slavery, some slaves escaped to the mountains to live independently, where they were known as Maroons.
Today, the famous Jamaican Blue Mountain Coffee, which commands premium prices on world markets, is cultivated between 2,000 and 5,000 feet above sea level, while higher slopes are preserved as forest.

Emancipation Park

Home

Emancipate yourself from slavery.

Nestled in the heart of urban New Kingston is one of Jamaica’s greatest historical treasures, Emancipation Park. It is themed a “Tribute to Freedom” and lives up to its theme visually with an 11ft bronze sculpture, named Redemption Song.
On July 31, 2002 Emancipation Park was officially opened in Kingston, Jamaica. The Park was created to be a symbol of our Freedom to Hope, to Excel and to Be. Emancipation Park is located in central New Kingston the home of corporate Jamaica. Visitors to the park can find some tranquility in the heart of corporate Jamaica.
If you’re a runner, there’s no better place to go for a jog. The park is encircled by a walking/running path. As you go around it, you might begin to sweat from the intensity of the tropical sun but will be immediately cooled by the welcome breeze.

Hope Zoo and Garden

Home

Roses and Daisies and . . Wildlife.

Located in the resort area of Kingston, the Hope Zoo is home to 900 animals across 60 species, including indigenous, endangered, and endemic animals. Among the main attractions is Lucas the Lion, which was a well anticipated addiiton to the animal population of the zoo.
In 1881, two hundred (200) acres of Hope Estate was purchased by the Government to establish an experimental garden. Fifty (50) acres of the land was devoted to the development and distribution of new varieties of sugar cane, and ten (10) acres for planting teak, Liberian Coffee, Trinidad Cocoa and pineapples.
During the last fifty years Hope Gardens has been through periods of care and neglect. During the period of care the Garden became a place for pleasure, recreation, sightseeing and picnicking. The Garden is especially enjoyed by visitors including students from the near by universities, who seek a quiet place to relax and study.
The Hope Botanical Gardens and the Hope Zoo are located on Old Hope Road in the parish of St. Andrew.
